# PNOT (Project Note) 📓
> **"Documentation as Code, Context as History."**

PNOT is a specialized, project-centric note-taking and documentation platform. It moves beyond loose documents, focusing on the technical lifecycle of projects and the seamless flow of data within a high-tech ecosystem.

## 🌟 The Philosophy (by Üstad)
Traditional note-taking apps are static. PNOT is dynamic—it is designed to be the "Technical Memory" of every project. By integrating directly with operational platforms like **QRVEE**, PNOT automatically generates and manages project notes, ensuring no technical detail is ever lost.

## 🔗 The QRVEE & MinWin Synergy
PNOT serves as the underlying technical layer for the entire ecosystem:
- **Automated Logging:** When a user completes a high-value contact or a technical milestone in QRVEE, PNOT automatically creates a "Project Note" capturing the frequency, conditions, and equipment data.
- **Equipment History:** Every device purchased through the **MinWin** marketplace automatically generates a technical life-log in PNOT. This includes serial numbers, initial maintenance notes, and transaction history.
- **Shared Membership:** Users enjoy a unified identity (SSO) across PNOT and QRVEE, allowing for seamless data portability between the "Operation" (QRVEE) and "Documentation" (PNOT) layers.

## 🚀 Key Workflows
- **Context-First Organization:** Notes are linked to specific technical projects, radio stations, or industrial commodities.
- **Developer-Friendly:** Designed with a "documentation-as-code" mentality, allowing for structured, searchable, and exportable technical archives.
- **Collaborative Wisdom:** Shared project spaces where teams (Radio Clubs, Industrial Units) can build a collective knowledge base.

## 🛠 Technical Architecture
- **Monorepo Ready:** Shares core logic and validation types with the broader ecosystem via `@qrv/shared`.
- **Real-Time Sync:** Powered by Firebase for instant cross-device updates.
- **Modular Data Structures:** Highly flexible schema designed to adapt to any technical project, from amateur radio antenna builds to industrial commodity logistics.
